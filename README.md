# Spiking Autoencoder for Video Compression

## Project Overview
This project focuses on utilizing spiking neural networks (SNNs) to implement an autoencoder for video compression. Spiking neural networks are a type of artificial neural network that more closely resembles biological neural networks than traditional models. They process information in the form of discrete events (spikes) instead of continuous signals, which can lead to more efficient representations and computations.

## Objectives
- To explore the capabilities of spiking autoencoders in the domain of video compression.
- To compare performance with traditional compression methods (e.g., H.264, VP9).
- To analyze the impact of different parameters on compression efficiency and quality.

## Methodology
1. **Data Preparation**: Collect and preprocess video datasets for training and evaluation.
2. **Model Design**: Design a spiking autoencoder architecture capable of encoding and decoding video frames.
3. **Training**: Train the model using appropriate loss functions and optimization techniques.
4. **Evaluation**: Assess the performance using metrics such as PSNR (Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio) and compression ratio.
